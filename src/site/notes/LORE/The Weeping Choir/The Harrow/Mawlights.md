<center>THE LIGHT OF DECEIT</center>

---

![MAWLIGHT_PORTRAIT.jpg](/img/user/IMAGES/MAWLIGHT_PORTRAIT.jpg)

“They are not stars.”

That was the first thing the woman said as she was dragged through the gates of [[LORE/The Land/Continents/Valndir, The Wandering Land/Regions/The Ashen Reaches/Settlements/Lasthearth\|Lasthearth]], blood crusted in the corners of her sockets, lips trembling with what fragments of language remained. “They are not stars. They do not shine.”

The Mawlights are among the most confounding manifestations of [[LORE/The Weeping Choir/The Harrow/The Harrow\|The Harrow]], the most quietly horrifying, the most still. They do not move. They do not scream. They do not chase. They simply… appear. Points of pale, blinding luminescence suspended in air. Perfect spheres of burning white, without texture or form. Even in the dead of night, they cast no shadow. Even beneath the sun, they are brighter.

Those who see them with the naked eye rarely survive. And those who do survive, do not return. Not truly.

The mind is not built for revelation. Those who lay eyes upon a Mawlight describe what follows not as pain, but as epiphany. They say it speaks in silence, pours meaning into them in a single instant and what is given cannot be contained. Victims claw at their faces, scream in languages they did not know, scribble spirals and sunbursts across walls until their fingers are down to bone. Some vomit blood. Some walk into fire, laughing as if the joke was on us all.

But most are found spread apart, torn limb from limb in patterns that seem… ritualistic. Spiraled. Geometrically precise. No sound is ever heard. No movement is ever recorded. And so we are left to ask is it the Mawlight itself that destroys them?

Or does it open a door?

There are whispers of those who can withstand it- at least for a time. The blind. The hollow. One survivor was a lone [[LORE/Factions/The Steel Apparitions\|Apparition]] who had long since burned out his sight, and claimed he heard the Mawlight as a "choir in bloom," yet stood firm. [[LORE/Factions/The First Magi\|The First Magi]]i speak of Veil-Wards and mirrorshields, but none have proven effective. A handful of iron-masked pilgrims of [[LORE/Factions/The Hollow Creed\|The Hollow Creed]] walk the wastes, praying for another encounter, claiming that "to look again is to understand it all."

None have returned.

They do not pursue. They are passive, fixed, and seemingly rooted to areas where the Veil is thinnest, crossroads of death, memory, and ruin. Most often, they are found in [[LORE/The Land/Continents/Kyndreth, The Birthplace/Regions/The Silent Plains/The Silent Plains\|The Silent Plains]], above cracked tombs, or hovering just inches above dry rivers that once flowed with divine blood. Some say they mark the graves of forgotten gods. Others believe they are not of the Choir at all, but remnants of something older, beacons of a reality that should never have been made.

Some say the Mawlights are the “eyes” of [[LORE/The Weeping Choir/The Weeping Choir\|The Weeping Choir]], sight given shape. Others think they are keys—each one unlocking a truth too vast, too hungry for a single soul to bear. I once thought them a warning.

Now, I am not so sure.

When I sleep, I see the light.
When I wake, I remember nothing but the shape of its silence.

And somewhere, across the wastes, they hang still in the sky.
Silent. Waiting.
They are not stars. They do not shine.
